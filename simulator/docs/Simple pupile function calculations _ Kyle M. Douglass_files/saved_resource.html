            Kyle M. Douglass Mod Vincent • a year ago

            Hi Vincent,
            I'm sorry for the late reply as I was away for a couple weeks.

            Yes, you are correct that the FFT assumes periodic boundary conditions. To be honest, I need to think about your question some more before I attempt a full response. In the meantime, I would highly recommend reading Chapter 2 of "Numerical Simulation of Optical Wave Propagation" by Jason D. Schmidt (http://spie.org/Publication... and specifically have a close look at Figures 2.6 and 2.7. Schmidt looks at the step-by-step evolution of an angular spectrum as you move away from the exact solution (given by the continuous Fourier transform) and towards the simulated solution (given by a discrete Fourier transform and a convolution that represents the effects of the image boundary). Deviations from the true solution are caused by a few different factors such as aliasing, the use of the discrete instead of continuous Fourier transform, and truncation by a square window function.

            I hope this helps,
            -kmd
